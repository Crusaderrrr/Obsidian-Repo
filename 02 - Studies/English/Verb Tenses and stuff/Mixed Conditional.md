**Mixed Conditionals** combine different conditional structures to talk about unreal past situations and their present results, or present situations and their past results.
Structure:
- Past → Present: If + Past Perfect, would + base verb.
- Present → Past: If + Past Simple, would have + past participle.
**Examples**:
- “If I had studied harder (past), I would be a doctor now (present).” (regret affecting today)
- “If she were more confident (present), she would have applied for the job (past).” (current state affecting a past decision)
- *Tip*: Use mixed conditionals when the time frames (past and present) overlap in a hypothetical way—great for advanced writing!
