The Past Perfect uses *had + past participle* to talk about an action that was completed before another past action or time.
- When to Use: To show the order of two past events (the earlier one gets Past Perfect).

**Examples**:
- “She had finished her homework before the movie started.” (Homework first, then movie.)
- “They had left by the time we arrived.” (Leaving happened earlier.)
- Tip: Use with “before,” “after,” or “by the time” to clarify sequence—great for storytelling or essays!
