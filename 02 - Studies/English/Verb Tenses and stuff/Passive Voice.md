The **Passive Voice** uses *be + past participle* to focus on the action or the receiver, not the doer.
**When to Use**: In formal writing or when the subject is unknown/less important.
**Examples**:
Active: “She wrote the report.” → Passive: “The report was written by her.”
Passive: “The decision has been made.” (No doer needed.)
**Tip**: Use in essays (e.g., “Research is conducted annually”)—great for C1!
