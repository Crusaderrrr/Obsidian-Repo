The Past Simple uses the past tense of the verb (regular: -ed; irregular: 2nd column) to talk about completed actions or events at a specific time in the past.

 **When to Use**: 
 For finished actions with a clear time (e.g., yesterday, last week).

**Examples**:
- “I visited Paris last year.” (specific time)
- “She wrote a letter yesterday.” (completed)
- “They went home at 6 PM.” (finished event)

*Use it when the time is known*—simple!
