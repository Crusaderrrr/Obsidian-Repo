The Present Perfect Continuous uses *have/has been + verb-ing* to show an action that started in the past and continues to be relevant now, often emphasizing duration or effort.

**When to Use**: For ongoing actions with a present impact or to highlight how long something has been happening.

Examples:
- “I have been studying English for months.” (Still studying, effort shown.)
- “She has been working late recently.” (Ongoing, affects now.)

Tip: Use “for” or “since” to mark time—great for describing your exam prep!
