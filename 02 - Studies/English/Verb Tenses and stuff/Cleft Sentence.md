A **cleft sentence** is a sentence that is split into two parts to emphasize a specific piece of information. It’s often used in spoken and written English to **highlight** or **stress** what’s important in a sentence.

There are different types of cleft sentences, but the most common ones are:

1. **It-cleft**
This uses the structure:  
👉 **It + be + emphasized information + relative clause**
 Examples:
- **It was Sarah** who broke the vase.  
    → (Normal sentence: _Sarah broke the vase._)
    
- **It is next Friday** that we’re having the meeting.  
    → (Normal: _We’re having the meeting next Friday._)
    
- **It was at the beach** where we first met.  
    → (Normal: _We first met at the beach._)
    

---
2. **What-cleft**
This starts with **"What"** and focuses on the action or idea.
 Examples:
- **What I need** is a long vacation.  
    → (Normal: _I need a long vacation._)
    
- **What made her angry** was the unfair decision.  
    → (Normal: _The unfair decision made her angry._)
    
- **What I like about this book** is the ending.  
    → (Normal: _I like the ending of this book._)
    

---
3. **All-cleft / Wh-clefts (who, where, why, etc.)**
These also begin with a **wh-word** and emphasize a part of the sentence.
 Examples:
- **All I want** is a bit of peace and quiet.
    
- **The person who helped me** was John.
    
- **Where I’d love to go** is Japan.
    

---
 **Why use cleft sentences?**
- To *highlight* new or important information.
    
- To make your speech or writing sound more *natural*, especially in storytelling or persuasive writing.
