What is Future Perfect Continuous?
It’s a tense used to talk about an action that: 
-  Will have been happening  
- For a period of time  
- Before or up to a specific moment in the future
- Often it is used with *By*: "By next month..."

**Structure**:
will have been + verb-ing

**Example:**
 By next year, I **will have been learning** English for three years.  
 This means:
- I started learning English in the past.
- I’m still learning now.
- In the future (next year), I will have spent 3 years learning.

 **When do we use it?**
We use it when we want to *emphasize the duration* of an action in the future.
**Duration = how long something will continue**

### ✅ More Examples:
- By 8 PM, she **will have been working** for 12 hours.
- When you arrive, they **will have been waiting** for an hour.
- Next month, I **will have been living** in Spain for 2 years.
