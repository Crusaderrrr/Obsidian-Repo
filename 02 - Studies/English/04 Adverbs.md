### Adverbs of Manner (describe how something happens)

**Meticulously** :: In a very careful and detailed way (She meticulously checked every detail)
<!--SR:!2025-04-12,2,194-->

**Briskly** :: Quickly and energetically (He walked briskly to catch the train)
<!--SR:!2025-04-12,2,194-->

**Profoundly** :: Deeply and intensely (She was profoundly moved by the speech)
<!--SR:!2025-04-15,5,234-->

**Vividly** :: In a clear and detailed way (I vividly remember my childhood home)
<!--SR:!2025-04-15,4,234-->
### Adverbs of Frequency (describe how often something happens)

**Infrequently** :: Not happening very often (We see each other infrequently nowadays)
<!--SR:!2025-04-15,5,234-->

**Intermittently** :: Happening at irregular intervals, not continuously (The signal drops intermittently)
<!--SR:!2025-04-12,1,154-->

**Routinely** :: Regularly, as part of a habit (She routinely works late)
<!--SR:!2025-04-12,2,194-->

**Sporadically** :: Occasionally, but not regularly (It rained sporadically throughout the day)
<!--SR:!2025-04-12,1,154-->


### Adverbs of Degree (describe to what extent something happens)

**Moderately** :: To a reasonable or average degree (She was moderately interested in the proposal)
<!--SR:!2025-04-12,2,194-->

**Utterly** :: Completely or absolutely (I was utterly exhausted after the trip)
<!--SR:!2025-04-12,1,154-->

**Considerably** :: By a large amount; significantly (The project has improved considerably)
<!--SR:!2025-04-14,4,214-->

**Decidedly** :: Without a doubt; definitely (That was decidedly the best meal I’ve had)
<!--SR:!2025-04-15,5,234-->


### Adverbs of Time (describe when something happens) 

**Subsequently** :: After something else has happened (She studied law and subsequently became a judge)
<!--SR:!2025-04-12,2,194-->

**Presently**(*) :: At the current time (She is presently working on a new book)
<!--SR:!2025-04-15,5,234-->

**Hitherto** :: Until this point in time (The results had been hitherto unknown)
<!--SR:!2025-04-12,1,154-->

**Prematurely** :: Too soon or before the right time (He prematurely announced the decision)
<!--SR:!2025-04-12,2,194-->


### Adverbs of Certainty (express how sure you are about something)

**Arguably** :: It can be supported with reasoning, but not everyone may agree (She is arguably the best candidate for the job)
<!--SR:!2025-04-15,5,234-->

**Presumably** :: Based on what is known, something is probably true (He is presumably on his way by now)
<!--SR:!2025-04-16,6,214-->

**Ostensibly** :: Appearing to be true, but possibly not (He was ostensibly working, but actually browsing online)
<!--SR:!2025-04-12,2,194-->

**Indisputably** :: Cannot be argued against; definitely true (This is indisputably a great achievement)
<!--SR:!2025-04-12,1,154-->

**Unquestionably** :: Beyond any doubt (She is unquestionably talented)
