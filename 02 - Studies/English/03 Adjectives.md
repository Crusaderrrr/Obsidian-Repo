**Unsafe**
?
Not safe; dangerous 
**Synonyms**: dangerous, risky, hazardous  
"The building was evacuated due to unsafe conditions"

**Nutritional**
?
Relating to the nutrients in food
**Synonyms**: dietary, nutritious, nourishing 
"It is important to consider the nutritional value of your diet"

**Gifted**
?
Having a special talent or ability 
**Synonyms**: talented, skilled, exceptional
"The school has programs for gifted children in the arts and science"

**Numerous**
?
Many in number
**Synonyms**: many, several, various 
"She has traveled to Paris numerous times"

**Notable**
?
Worthy of attention; important
**Synonyms**: remarkable, significant, noteworthy
"Her notable achievements in science earned her several awards"

**Mediocre**
?
Not very good; average
**Synonyms**: average, *ordinary*, so-so
"His performance was *mediocre*, not meeting the expectations"

**Inappropriate**
?
Not suitable or proper
**Synonyms**: unsuitable, improper, unfit
"His inappropriate behavior at the meeting was not acceptable"

**Insufficient**
?
Not enough 
**Synonyms**: inadequate, lacking, deficient 
"The project was cancelled due to insufficient funds"

**Informative** 
?
Providing useful information 
“The lecture was very informative and helped me understand the topic better”

**Affluent**
?
Having a lot of money 
**Synonyms**: wealthy, rich, prosperous 
Affluent society/country; *become affluent* (successful) 
"The affluent society had may beautiful homes and parks" 

**Accountable**
?
Responsible for your actions 
**Synonyms**: responsible, answerable, liable
Accountable to / for 
"Managers should be held accountable for their decisions"

**Detrimental** 
? 
Causing harm or damage
**Synonyms**: harmful, damaging, bad
Detrimental effect
"Smoking has a detrimental effect on health"

**Fundamental**
?
Important 
**Synonyms**: important, essential, primary 
Fundamental principal/change 
“Learning to read is a fundamental skill for children”

**Prone**
?
Likely to do or experience something 
**Synonyms**: likely, inclined, susceptible 
Prone to errors 
“We are prone to making mistakes when learning”

**Prime** 
?
Best or most important
**Synonyms**: best, top, main 
Prime advantage / disadvantage / reason / *example* 
“This is the prime example of a well constructed web site”

**Viable** 
?
Able to work or succeed 
**Synonyms**: feasible, workable, possible 
Viable *option*/*alternative*/*solution* 
“A viable solution is to heavily punish lawbreakers”

**Certain** 
? 
The analog of *sure*: "Are you **certain** we are in the right place?"
Some particular not named thing or prsn: "**Certain** people just don't understand smth"
Fixed/defined: "There are **certain** rules you must know"


**Hostile** :: unfriendly, antagonistic 

**Outstanding**
?
Excellent or exceptional: “An outstanding job”
Not yet resolved or completed: “Outstanding debts”
*Synonyms*: excellent, extraordinary, **remarkable**

**Paramount**
?
that stays first or needed to be done first
Example: “Your work is paramount, then you do what you want”
